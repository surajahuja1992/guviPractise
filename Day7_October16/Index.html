SCROLLING IN WEB DESIGN THE LONG & THE SHORT OF IT
LONG SCROLL: THE GOOD STUFF VISUAL CREATIVITY

There's more visual freedom in parallax website design. Rather than focusing on block style information architecture, you can create refreshing and gngaging visuals such as animations or beautiful photography, that act as a continumm, ultimately inspiring users to scroll further down the page

S TORY-TELLING  OPPORTUNITIES

Do you want to tell a stroy with you product or service? Long-scrolling lends itself well to a narrative scrolling and encourages meaningful interaction from users.

S  IMPLE INTERFACE FOR SMART DEVICE USERS

Mobile and tablets have envolved the way we engage in websites. It's now less about clicking and more about scrolling,swiping and tapping. Long-scrolling websites take away the barrier of complex navigation, providing a responsive,user-friendly experience for mobile users.

P UNCHY, STRAIGHT TOTHE POINT MESSAGING

Parallax website design is a great solution for us time-poor websites browsers,as it focuses business to convey their message and vision into ine dynamic web design page.

F ASIER FOR USERS TOREALISE THEIR GOAL

These days,scrolling is so simple that its become an instinctive action,whereas clicking though to another page requires thought and motivation.

L ONG SCROLL: THE NOT SO GOOD STUFF
YOU NEED TO BE SEARCH ENGINE SAVVY

One of the biggest problems that parallax websites face is that they are not so SEO friendly.Why? When you limit the number of pages on your website,Google has fewer pages to index and negatively impacts your standing

S LOWER LOADS TIMES

50% of websites use bounce off the page if it hasnt loaded in 3-5 seconds. So the more high resolution imagery and animations you include, the longer it takes to load the page. When writing this artice, we tested somepopular parallax websites,and found that one with complexvisuals took upto 30 seconds to load on a mobile device,whereas simple long-scrolling websites loaded within a few seconds

A WKWARD NAVIGATION

Instead of hitting the "back" button, users must scroll back up to the page which can be frustrating for the users who want to refer back to a certain element. An easy way around this is to include top navigation and have each element anchored to a category or section
